Urgent processing (1–3 days)
Priority Processing

For urgent travel needs. Tatkal scheme allows faster processing with additional fees and a verification certificate from a Gazetted Officer.

  • All Fresh Passport documents
  • Tatkal declaration by Gazetted Officer
  • Proof of urgent travel (air ticket / medical emergency)
  • Self-declaration of genuineness

Re-issue Service

  • Original old / expired passport
  • Aadhaar Card

  • 2 recent passport-size photographs

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get a passport? ▾
Under Normal scheme: 30–45 working days after PSK visit. Under Tatkal scheme: 7–14 working days. Timing depends on police verification and regional passport office workload.
Is Aadhaar mandatory for passport application? ▾
Aadhaar is the most widely accepted document for address and identity proof. While it is not technically mandatory, submitting Aadhaar significantly speeds up the process. Alternatively, Voter ID, bank passbook, or utility bills can be used.
Can I apply for a passport if I have changed my name? ▾
Yes. You need to submit a Gazette Notification of the name change, along with a newspaper advertisement and an affidavit. Our team can guide you through this process step by step.
Do you provide services across India? ▾
Yes, e-filling.in provides online facilitation services for passport applications across all states and union territories of India. Our online process means you can get started from anywhere without visiting our office.
What is your refund policy? ▾
Government fees paid to Passport Seva Kendra are non-refundable as per Government of India policy. Our service fee is refundable under specific conditions — please refer to our full Refund Policy for details.
